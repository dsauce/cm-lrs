## Use of proceeds

Net proceeds to the Issuer (estimated USD 343m after transaction costs, assuming no exercise of the greenshoe) will be applied to:

1. **Funding of capital expenditure** — approximately USD 180m towards the build-out of the Issuer's third-generation cryogenic-photonic processor production line at its Linköping (Sweden) facility, with commissioning targeted for H2 2027;
2. **Strategic capped call transactions** — approximately USD 28m to finance the cost of the capped call described below;
3. **General corporate purposes**, including working capital, opportunistic talent and IP acquisitions, and continued R&D investment in error-corrected quantum processors.

---

## Capped call transactions

Concurrently with the offering, the Issuer expects to enter into capped call transactions with one or more of the Initial Purchasers or their affiliates. The capped calls are generally expected to reduce the potential dilution to the Issuer's underlying equity upon conversion of the Notes and / or offset any cash payments the Issuer is required to make in excess of the principal amount of converted Notes.

The capped calls are expected to have a strike price equal to the initial conversion price of the Notes (USD 37.63) and a cap price approximately **75% above the reference share price** (i.e. approximately USD 49.70).

---

## Conversion rights

Holders may convert their Notes at any time prior to the close of business on the second scheduled trading day immediately preceding the maturity date under any of the following circumstances:

1. **Sale price condition** — during any calendar quarter commencing after 31 December 2026, if the last reported sale price of the underlying share equivalent per ADR for at least 20 trading days during the 30 consecutive trading day period ending on the last trading day of the immediately preceding calendar quarter is greater than or equal to **130%** of the conversion price on each applicable trading day.
2. **Trading price condition** — during the 5 business days immediately following any 10 consecutive trading day period in which the trading price per USD 1,000 principal amount of the Notes for each trading day was less than **98%** of the product of the last reported sale price of the underlying share equivalent and the conversion rate.
3. **Specified corporate events** — including spin-offs, distributions of dividends in excess of an agreed threshold, and fundamental changes (which trigger investor put and conversion-rate adjustment).
4. **After 22 July 2031** — at the holder's option, regardless of any other condition.

---

## Investor put on year 3

On **22 October 2029** (the third anniversary of the issue date), each holder may require the Issuer to repurchase its Notes at **100% of principal** plus accrued and unpaid interest.

---

## Optional redemption by the Issuer

The Issuer may, after 22 October 2028 (two years after issue date), redeem all or part of the Notes at **100% of principal** plus accrued and unpaid interest, **provided that** the last reported sale price of the underlying share equivalent has equalled or exceeded **130% of the conversion price** for at least 20 trading days during any 30 consecutive trading day period ending within 5 trading days of the redemption notice.

A holder receiving a redemption notice may elect to convert its Notes (instead of accepting the redemption) and will receive, in addition to the conversion consideration, a **make-whole premium** in the form of additional shares calculated by reference to the make-whole table set out in the indenture.

---

## Fundamental change

Upon a "fundamental change" (defined to include a change of control of the Issuer; the sale of substantially all assets; the cessation of listing of the Issuer's underlying equity; or a shareholder-approved liquidation or dissolution), each holder may require the Issuer to repurchase its Notes at **100% of principal** plus accrued and unpaid interest.

A holder who instead elects to convert during a fundamental change is entitled to a **make-whole adjustment** to the conversion rate calculated by reference to the date of the change and the share price implied by the transaction.

---

## Anti-dilution adjustments

Standard adjustments to the conversion rate for:

- Share splits, share consolidations and share dividends;
- Issuance of rights or warrants below the prevailing trading price;
- Spin-offs and distributions of capital stock or assets to shareholders;
- Cash dividends exceeding a quarterly threshold of USD 0.05 per underlying share equivalent;
- Tender or exchange offers at a premium to market.

## Events of default

1. Non-payment (10 business day grace period for interest);
2. Failure to convert in accordance with the indenture;
3. Failure to deliver fundamental-change repurchase consideration;
4. Cross-acceleration to financial indebtedness exceeding USD 50m;
5. Insolvency events affecting the Issuer or any Material Subsidiary;
6. Invalidity or repudiation of the indenture.

## Risk factors (representative, abbreviated)

- The Issuer is at an early commercial stage; substantial losses are expected to continue for several years.
- Quantum-computing demand is dependent on enterprise-buyer adoption that has historically progressed in fits and starts.
- Concentration risk: top three customers represent approximately 47% of FY2025 revenue, including two US-based hyperscale cloud providers.
- Export-control risk: the Issuer's processors may be subject to dual-use export controls in Sweden, the European Union and the United States.
- Equity-dilution risk on conversion: assuming full conversion at the initial conversion price, the Notes would represent approximately 6.2% dilution to the existing share capital before the impact of the capped calls.
- Geopolitical, ESG, and operational risks customary for a Nordic technology issuer with a global customer base.
