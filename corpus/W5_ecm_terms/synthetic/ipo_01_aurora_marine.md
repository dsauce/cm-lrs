## Use of proceeds

The net proceeds to the Issuer from the primary tranche (estimated NOK 2,310m after transaction costs) will be applied to:

1. **Capital expenditure on fleet expansion** — approximately NOK 1,500m towards the contracted delivery of two next-generation installation vessels with deeper-water capability (water depths to 80m), scheduled for delivery 2027 and 2028;
2. **Debt repayment** — repayment of approximately NOK 600m of the Issuer's existing senior secured bank facility, leaving the Issuer with pro forma net leverage of approximately 1.8x EBITDA at completion;
3. **General corporate purposes**, including working capital and project-bidding capacity for tendered offshore-wind installation contracts in the UK, German and Polish markets.

The Selling Shareholder receives proceeds from the secondary tranche (estimated NOK 1,420m at the mid-point of the price range, after transaction costs). The Issuer does not receive any of those proceeds.

## Stabilisation

The Stabilisation Manager may, on behalf of the Underwriters, undertake transactions to stabilise the market price of the shares for a period of up to 30 calendar days from the first day of trading. Any stabilisation transactions will be conducted in accordance with EU Regulation 596/2014 (Market Abuse Regulation) and applicable Norwegian regulations.

The Stabilisation Manager is not obliged to undertake stabilisation transactions; there is no assurance that stabilisation transactions will be undertaken; and if undertaken, they may be discontinued at any time without notice.

## Risk factors (representative, abbreviated)

- **Project pipeline risk** — the Issuer's revenue is concentrated in a small number of multi-year vessel charter contracts; loss of any single contract may materially affect FY-level revenue and EBITDA.
- **Vessel delivery risk** — newbuild vessels are subject to shipyard schedule slippage; a material delay in either of the two contracted deliveries (2027 / 2028) would defer the revenue uplift assumed in this offering's pricing.
- **Regulatory and capacity-market risk** — offshore-wind project sanctioning depends on contracts-for-difference and equivalent regimes; political or regulatory changes in the UK, German and Polish markets may delay or cancel projects.
- **Cyclical exposure** — installation revenues are correlated to wind-farm capex cycles which are themselves correlated to power prices and the cost of capital.
- **Concentration risk** — top three customers represent approximately 64% of FY2025 revenue.
- **Working capital and contract-loss risk** — fixed-price installation contracts expose the Issuer to cost overrun risk; the Issuer's standard contract terms include limited indexation clauses, which are described in the prospectus.

## Lock-up exceptions, governance and other matters

- **Pre-emption rights:** the Issuer's articles preserve statutory pre-emption rights under the Norwegian Public Limited Liability Companies Act; the Annual General Meeting will be asked at the first AGM post-listing for a customary authorisation to issue up to 10% of the share capital without pre-emption, valid for two years.
- **Voting structure:** one share, one vote (no dual-class structure).
- **Board composition at listing:** 7 directors, of whom 5 are independent within the meaning of the Norwegian Code of Practice for Corporate Governance.
- **Dividend policy:** the Issuer intends to recommend a dividend of 30–50% of consolidated net income from FY2026 onwards, subject to leverage remaining below 2.5x net debt / EBITDA and adequate distributable reserves.
